# Personalized Longevity Protocols

Based on weight, health conditions, and biomarkers — optimized for practical daily use.

## Body Composition Profiles

### Profile A: Lean (BMI < 22, < 15% body fat)
**Goal:** Maintain muscle mass, prevent age-related atrophy

| Factor | Recommendation |
|--------|----------------|
| **Protein** | 1.6-2.0 g/kg/day (higher than average — protect muscle) |
| **Resistance Training** | 3x/week minimum, focus on progressive overload |
| **mTOR Cycling** | Less aggressive fasting — you're already lean |
| **Creatine** | Essential — muscle protein synthesis aid |
| **VO2 Max Focus** | Zone 2 base + HIIT for mitochondrial density |

**Supplements:**
- Creatine: 5g/day
- Vitamin D3: 3000 IU
- Omega-3: 2g EPA/DHA
- Magnesium: 400mg
- Whey or meat-based protein with leucine (2.5-3g leucine/meal)

---

### Profile B: Average (BMI 22-27, 15-25% body fat)
**Goal:** Metabolic optimization, prevent middle-age spread

| Factor | Recommendation |
|--------|----------------|
| **Protein** | 1.4-1.6 g/kg/day |
| **Zone 2 Cardio** | 3-4 hrs/week |
| **Strength** | 2-3x/week |
| **TRE** | 14:10 window most days |
| **GLP-1 Consideration** | If BMI > 25 with metabolic issues, discuss with doctor |

**Supplements:**
- Vitamin D3: 3000 IU
- Omega-3: 1.5g EPA/DHA
- Magnesium: 400mg
- Creatine: 3-5g/day
- Berberine: 500mg 2x day (glucose metabolism)

---

### Profile C: Overweight (BMI 27-32, > 25% body fat)
**Goal:** Fat loss, improve metabolic markers, reduce inflammation

| Factor | Recommendation |
|--------|----------------|
| **Protein** | 1.6-2.0 g/kg/day (preserve lean mass during deficit) |
| **Caloric Deficit** | 300-500 kcal/day — sustainable, not aggressive |
| **Zone 2 Cardio** | 4-5 hrs/week — high volume, low intensity |
| **Strength** | 3x/week — critical for body composition |
| **TRE** | 16:8 or 18:6 for metabolic switching |
| **Sleep** | 8+ hours — poor sleep = increased hunger hormones |

**Supplements:**
- Omega-3: 2-3g EPA/DHA (anti-inflammatory)
- Vitamin D3: 5000 IU
- Magnesium: 400-600mg
- Creatine: 5g/day (supports metabolism, preserves muscle)
- Berberine: 500mg 3x day (insulin sensitivity)
- Caffeine: 100-200mg before exercise (fat oxidation)

---

### Profile D: Obese (BMI > 32, > 30% body fat)
**Goal:** Significant metabolic improvement, prioritize cardiovascular risk reduction

| Factor | Recommendation |
|--------|----------------|
| **Medical Supervision** | Strongly recommended |
| **Protein** | 1.8-2.2 g/kg/day (high to preserve muscle) |
| **Zone 2** | Start with 2-3 hrs/week, build up |
| **Strength** | 2-3x/week from day 1 |
| **GLP-1 Agonists** | Highly recommended if available (Semaglutide, Tirzepatide) |
| **Tre** | 16:8 minimum, build to 20:4 if tolerated |
| **Priority Metrics** | Fasting insulin, HS-CRP, HbA1c, lipids |

**Supplements:**
- Omega-3: 3g EPA/DHA
- Vitamin D3: 5000 IU
- Magnesium: 400-600mg
- Creatine: 5g/day
- Berberine: 500mg 3x day
- CoQ10: 100-200mg (support cardiovascular)

## Health Condition Protocols

### Type 2 Diabetes / Insulin Resistance
**Primary Goal:** Insulin sensitivity, glucose control

| Time | Action |
|------|--------|
| Morning | Berberine 500mg with breakfast OR Metformin (doctor-prescribed) |
| With Each Meal | 1.6g/kg protein, prioritize chicken/fish/egg whites |
| Post-Meal | 15-min walk (reduces glucose spike by 30%) |
| Evening | Magnesium glycinate 400mg (insulin sensitivity) |
| 3x/week | Resistance training (muscle is glucose disposal tissue) |

**Key supplements:** Berberine, Chromium, Alpha-lipoic acid, Omega-3
**Avoid:** Fruit juice, white bread, sugary foods, seed oils
**Target labs:** Fasting insulin < 5, HbA1c < 5.4, HOMA-IR < 1.0

---

### Hypertension
**Primary Goal:** Endothelial health, reduce cardiovascular risk

| Time | Action |
|------|--------|
| Morning | Beetroot powder or beetroot juice (NO for low BP) |
| With Breakfast | Taurine 1-2g, Magnesium 400mg |
| Daily | 4-7 sauna sessions if available |
| 3x/week | Zone 2 cardio (30-45 min) |
| Evening | Avoid high sodium, prioritize potassium-rich foods |

**Key supplements:** Magnesium, Taurine, Beetroot (NO2), Omega-3, CoQ10
**Food focus:** Leafy greens, beets, bananas, sardines, olive oil
**Target:** < 120/80 mmHg, HS-CRP < 1.0

---

### High Cholesterol / Dyslipidemia
**Primary Goal:** LDL reduction, HDL optimization, triglyceride control

| Time | Action |
|------|--------|
| Morning | Berberine 500mg (reduces LDL ~20%) |
| With Meals | Omega-3 2-3g, Plant sterols |
| Daily | 30+ grams fiber (oats, beans, psyllium) |
| 3x/week | Zone 2 + strength |
| Weekly | 2+ servings fatty fish (salmon, sardines, mackerel) |

**Key supplements:** Berberine, Omega-3, Red yeast rice (if no statin), Plant sterols
**Food focus:** Fatty fish, olive oil, nuts, oats, beans, avocados
**Target:** LDL < 100 (optimal < 70), TG < 100, HDL > 50

| Time | Action |

**Protein sources:** Whey, beef, chicken, eggs, fish, casein
**Target:** Grip strength > 40kg (men), > 25kg (women); VO2 max improving

## Age-Based Modifications

### Ages 25-40
- Focus: Building habits, exercise intensity OK
- Protein: 1.6-2.0 g/kg
- Priority: VO2 max development, muscle building
- Key concern: Sedentary lifestyle creeping in

### Ages 40-55
- Focus: Hormonal changes, metabolic shift
- Protein: 1.6-2.2 g/kg (muscle harder to maintain)
- Priority: Strength training, Zone 2 cardio
- Key concerns: Cortisol management, sleep quality

### Ages 55-70
- Focus: Preservation, healthspan optimization
- Protein: 2.0-2.4 g/kg (anabolic resistance)
- Priority: Strength > cardio, flexibility, balance
- Key concerns: Sarcopenia, bone density, cognitive health

### Ages 70+
- Focus: Quality of life, independence
- Protein: 2.2-2.5 g/kg with leucine at each meal
- Priority: Daily movement, social connection, purpose
- Key concerns: Falls, isolation, polypharmacy

## Extended Intake Questionnaire

The protocols above work well with basic info, but adding these data points makes personalization dramatically more powerful.

### Sleep & Chronotype

| Question | Why It Changes the Protocol |
|----------|----------------------------|
| What time do you naturally wake/sleep (no alarm)? | Determines circadian anchor, meal timing, supplement scheduling |
| Do you feel worse with early mornings or later starts? | Chronotype affects when to take stimulants (caffeine), when to exercise |
| Hours until you feel fully alert after waking? | Sleep inertia = potential need for morning light, alertness supps |
| Do you snore or stop breathing during sleep? | Could signal sleep apnea → affects cardiovascular protocols |
| Average sleep hours? Consistency (weekend drift)? | Inconsistent sleep = disrupted ghrelin/leptin, insulin sensitivity |

**Protocol adjustments based on this:**
- Night owls: Shift exercise and meals later; take morning supplements later
- Early birds: Front-load calories earlier; caffeine OK by 6am
- Sleep apnea: Add CoQ10, prioritize nasal breathing exercises, consider IGF-1 optimization

---

### Stress & Mental Load

| Question | Why It Changes the Protocol |
|----------|----------------------------|
| How would you rate your chronic stress (1-10)? | High cortisol = prioritize ashwagandha, magnesium, reduced HIIT |
| Do you have racing thoughts at night? | Adds L-theanine, apigenin, GABA support |
| Do you have moments of genuine joy/laughter daily? | Social connection is a longevity pillar — affects protocol prioritization |
| Do you meditate or have a mindfulness practice? | If no, stress management becomes higher priority than additional supplements |
| Are you in a constant state of deadline pressure? | Elevated cortisol needs: phosphatidylserine, magnesium glycinate, morning sun |

**Protocol adjustments:**
- High stress (7+/10): Reduce HIIT, prioritize Zone 2 + walking, add adaptogens
- Racing mind: Increase evening magnesium, add glycine, GABA support
- Low joy/connection: Add social prescription, prioritize omega-3s for mood

---

### Digestive Health

| Question | Why It Changes the Protocol |
|----------|----------------------------|
| How many bowel movements per day/week? | Slow transit = more time for toxins, bacterial overgrowth risk |
| Do you bloat after meals? | Could signal low stomach acid, dysbiosis, or food intolerances |
| Do you feel sleepy after large meals? | Post-meal fatigue = blood sugar dysregulation or low stomach acid |
| Are you currently taking a PPI or antacid? | PPIs deplete B12, magnesium, calcium — need replenishment |
| Do you have food intolerances (gluten, dairy, eggs)? | Removes those protein sources from recommendations |
| Do you eat the same foods repeatedly? | Low diversity = microbiome depletion — add 30 plants/week target |

**Protocol adjustments:**
- Bloating/low stomach acid: Add HCL betaine with meals, reduce protein from dairy
- PPI use: Add B12, magnesium, calcium, and work with doctor to reduce PPI if possible
- Constipation: Increase magnesium, fiber, hydration, add movement

---

### Activity & Movement

| Question | Why It Changes the Protocol |
|----------|----------------------------|
| Steps per day on average (rough estimate)? | Sedentary baseline affects how much Zone 2 is additive vs recovery |
| What is your job (desk, standing, physical)? | Determines whether training volume needs to compensate for or complement work movement |
| Do you exercise fasted or fed? | Fasted training affects glycogen depletion, recovery, and supplement timing |
| Do you have joint pain or movement limitations? | Changes exercise prescription — swimming vs running, push/pull/legs split |
| What type of exercise have you done consistently in the past? | Past success indicates what you'll likely stick to |

**Protocol adjustments:**
- Sedentary job + evening exerciser: More Zone 2 needed, reduce training intensity
- Physical labor job: Prioritize recovery over volume, reduce supplemental stimulants
- Joint issues: Prioritize swimming/cycling, add collagen, glucosamine

### Diet & Nutrition Patterns

| Question | Why It Changes the Protocol |
|----------|----------------------------|
| Are you vegan, vegetarian, or eat meat? | Affects protein sources, B12, iron, zinc, creatine, DHA |
| Do you eat breakfast or do you prefer fasting? | First meal time shifts the entire daily protocol |
| How much water do you drink daily? | Hydration affects everything — electrolytes change with >3L water |
| Do you drink alcohol (frequency, amount)? | Alcohol depletes zinc, B1, folate; disrupts sleep architecture |
| Do you consume caffeine? How much and when? | Half-life varies by genetics (CYP1A2); last dose timing changes |
| Do you eat organ meats, bone broth, or fermented foods? | Traditional foods = gut and nutrient density — may reduce supplement needs |
| What oils do you cook with? | Seed oils = inflammatory; affects omega-6:3 ratio target |

**Protocol adjustments:**
- Vegan/vegetarian: Add creatine, B12, zinc, iron, DHA/EPA, lysine (for protein complement)
- No breakfast / late eating: Compress the eating window, adjust morning supplement timing
- Heavy alcohol use: Add NAC, milk thistle, B-complex, zinc before alcohol
- Heavy caffeine use: Check last dose — if >6hrs before bed, still may disrupt sleep

### Current Supplement & Medication Stack

| Question | Why It Matters |
|----------|----------------|
| What supplements are you currently taking? | Avoid duplication (e.g., already taking fish oil + omega-3 = too much) |
| Are you on any prescription medications? | Drug-nutrient interactions (statins + CoQ10, Metformin + B12) |
| Have you done recent blood work? What markers? | Allows targeting specific numbers vs general ranges |
| Do you take any over-the-counter meds regularly? | NSAIDs deplete stomach acid and gut lining; melatonin can suppress T3 |

**Common interactions to flag:**
- Statins → Take CoQ10 (ubiquinol preferred)
- Metformin → Take B12 and magnesium
- Blood pressure meds → May need potassium monitoring
- PPIs → Take B12, magnesium, calcium
- Thyroid meds → Take zinc, selenium, iron separately; avoid calcium near dose

### Health Goals & Priorities

| Question | Why It Changes the Protocol |
|----------|----------------------------|
| What's your #1 priority right now: energy, fat loss, muscle, sleep, or longevity? | One goal usually needs to be the anchor — trying to optimize everything at once dilutes focus |
| Is there a specific event or deadline driving this? | Short-term vs long-term focus changes protocol aggressiveness |
| How much time per day can you realistically invest in health? | If 10 min: prioritize supplements + sleep. If 90 min: full protocol |
| Do you want to add years to your life, or healthspan to the years you have? | Shapes the intensity of interventions |

**Protocol adjustments:**
- Energy focus: Prioritize iron/ferritin check, B12, Vitamin D, thyroid panel, morning light
- Fat loss focus: Prioritize Zone 2, protein, berberine, GLP-1 consideration if eligible
- Muscle focus: Prioritize protein, creatine, progressive overload, sleep
- Longevity focus: Multi-modal — exercise, diet, sleep, social, purposeful

### Environmental & Lifestyle Factors

| Question | Why It Changes the Protocol |
|----------|----------------------------|
| What city/climate do you live in? | Cold = brown fat activation, more D3 needed. Heat = hydration + electrolytes |
| What is your altitude? | >5000ft = more EPO needed, adjust exercise recovery |
| Do you have sun exposure daily? | Determines how much Vitamin D you make endogenously |
| Are you regularly exposed to toxins (construction, painting, chemicals)? | Increases need for glutathione support, NAC, milk thistle |
| What is your social support structure for health? | Accountability partner = significantly higher protocol adherence |
| Do you travel frequently across time zones? | Affects circadian protocols, melatonin timing |

**Protocol adjustments:**
- Low sun/low latitude: Increase Vitamin D3 dose (5000-10000 IU)
- High toxin exposure: Add NAC 600mg, selenium, turmeric, increase glutathione precursors
- Frequent travel: Circadian reset protocol with melatonin, light exposure, fasting
- Strong social support: Less friction for protocol adherence — leverage it

## Putting It All Together: Priority Order

When a person provides their full intake, optimize in this order:

1. **Sleep** — Fixing sleep unlocks everything else (hormones, recovery, hunger hormones)
2. **Stress & nervous system** — Chronic stress undermines all other interventions
3. **Protein & amino acids** — Muscle is the longevity organ; protein is non-negotiable
4. **Movement** — Zone 2 + resistance training, adapted to their baseline
5. **Targeted supplements** — Based on diet gaps, health conditions, labs
6. **Advanced interventions** — Fasting protocols, nootropics, peptides (only after basics are covered)
